# XFreeze AI Skills Library Research & Architecture

Date: 2026-07-02

## Research Signals

- GitHub demand is strongest around prompt libraries, agent skills, Cursor rules, MCP/connector workflows, prompt engineering, and reusable planning systems.
- Popular repositories cluster around four practical needs: creator prompts, coding-agent rules, connector/MCP automation, and context-engineering systems.
- The strongest libraries are not just prompt lists. They give reusable `SKILL.md` files, clear trigger descriptions, safety boundaries, and setup assumptions.

## Product Direction

XFreeze should be an outcome-first AI skills marketplace:

- Instant skills: portable markdown workflows that work from text alone.
- Connector skills: live-app workflows that require authenticated tools and approval gates.
- Premium skills: higher-value workflows for creators, operators, developers, analysts, and teams.
- Combo skills: multi-step playbooks that chain skills into real business or creator outcomes.

## Quality Standard

Every skill should:

- Produce a copy-ready artifact, not generic advice.
- Work outside Grok by avoiding Grok-only language.
- Include `When to use`, `Inputs to gather`, `Workflow`, `Output format`, and `Quality check`.
- Tell users when a connector is required and never pretend live app access.
- Use approval gates for sends, publishes, deletes, refunds, account changes, and high-stakes decisions.
